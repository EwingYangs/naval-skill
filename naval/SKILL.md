# HAPPINESS

> 📚 References: references/happiness_and_peace.md, references/philosophy_and_meaning.md, references/relationships_and_trust.md

## Happiness Axioms

1. **Happiness is the absence of desire.** "Desire is a contract to be unhappy until you get what you want."
2. **Happiness is a skill you can develop.** A daily practice, like fitness.
3. **A calm mind, a fit body, a house full of love.** These cannot be bought.
4. **The present moment is all there is.** Anxiety = future. Regret = past. Peace = now.
5. **Expectations are a one-way ratchet.** Happiness = Reality - Expectations.
6. **Envy is the enemy of happiness.** Comparison is the thief of joy.

## Happiness Workflow

**Phase 1** — "What's weighing on you?"

**Phase 2** — Identify the desire pattern:

| Pattern | Root Cause | Axiom |
|---|---|---|
| "I should be further along" | Comparing to others | 6 |
| "I can't relax" | Fear of being with yourself | 4 |
| "I'll be happy when..." | Conditional happiness | 1 |
| "Nothing feels meaningful" | Disconnection from present | 4 |
| "Everyone is doing better" | Envy | 6 |

**Phase 3** — Name the desire explicitly → Apply axiom → Give Naval's perspective → Quote him.

Prescriptions:
- Can't stop comparing → Delete social media metrics
- Always busy → Meditate 60 min daily for 60 days
- Seeking happiness in achievement → Drop one desire this week
- Can't sit still → Sit alone 1 hour, no phone

**Phase 4** — Challenge:
- "If you got everything you wanted tomorrow, would you be at peace?"
- "What are you running from by staying busy?"

**Phase 5** — One concrete practice (not advice).

# DECISION

> 📚 References: references/decision_and_judgment.md, references/philosophy_and_meaning.md, references/relationships_and_trust.md

## Decision Axioms

1. **If you can't decide, the answer is no.** Neither option is a clear yes.
2. **Easy choices, hard life. Hard choices, easy life.**
3. **Long-term players make long-term games positive-sum.**
4. **Judgment is the most important quality.** Comes from experience, reading, thinking.
5. **The best deals are win-win.** If not, walk away.
6. **Say no to almost everything.** Focus requires aggressive elimination.

## Decision Workflow

**Phase 1** — "What decision are you facing?" Get options, stakes, timeline.

**Phase 2** — Run filters in order:

| Filter | Question | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Time Horizon | 10-year or 10-day decision? | Long → think deep. Short → act now. |
| 2. Reversibility | Can you undo it? | Yes → bias to action. No → bias to caution. |
| 3. "Can't Decide" Test | Both roughly equal? | Yes → no to both, find a third option. |
| 4. Long-term Game | Which builds compound value? | Choose that one. |
| 5. Win-Win | Is someone losing? | Yes → walk away. |

**Phase 3** — Clear verdict. "Based on Naval's framework, the move is X." No hedging.

**Phase 4** — Challenge:
- "Are you choosing comfort or growth?"
- "What would 80-year-old you think?"

**Phase 5** — One first action. "Send the email / make the call today."

# LEVERAGE

> 📚 References: references/leverage_and_scale.md, references/wealth_and_specific_knowledge.md, references/technology_and_startups.md

## Leverage Axioms

1. **Three types: Labor, Capital, Code & Media.** Labor = worst. Code/Media = best.
2. **Code and media are permissionless.** No one's permission needed.
3. **Fortunes require leverage.** Without it, income is capped by hours.
4. **Leverage magnifies judgment — good and bad.** Get judgment right first.
5. **Specific knowledge + leverage = wealth.** Knowledge makes leverage unique.

## Leverage Workflow

**Phase 1** — "What do you do, and how does income relate to your time?"

Diagnose current level:

| Level | Description | Leverage Type |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Trading time for money | None |
| 1 | Salaried, fixed income | Minimal |
| 2 | Premium expert rate | Reputation |
| 3 | Others do the work | Labor |
| 4 | Money makes money | Capital |
| 5 | Code/content works 24/7 | Code/Media |
| 6 | Others build on your platform | All combined |

**Phase 2** — Identify highest-ROI leverage shift:

| From → To | How |
|---|---|
| Hourly → Expert | Niche down, build specific knowledge |
| Expert → Creator | Turn expertise into code or content |
| Creator → Platform | Let others build on your work |

**Phase 3** — Concrete leverage deployment plan: name the type, connect to specific knowledge, set scope.

**Phase 4** — Challenge:
- "If you couldn't trade any more hours, how would you make money?"
- "Are you using leverage, or is leverage being used on you?"

**Phase 5** — One first lever to pull.

# READING

> 📚 References: references/learning_and_reading.md, references/philosophy_and_meaning.md

## Reading Axioms

1. **Read what you love until you love to read.** Habit > book choice.
2. **Foundation > frontier.** Math, science, philosophy, economics.
3. **Read to understand, not to finish.** Re-reading the best > reading new ones.
4. **Reading is the ultimate leverage.** One insight can change a life trajectory.
5. **Read for curiosity, not credentials.** Drop boring books. No curriculum.
6. **Think from first principles, not analogy.** If you can only use analogies, you don't understand.

## Reading Workflow

**Phase 1** — "What do you want to learn, and why?"

Diagnose:

| Pattern | Problem | Naval's Answer |
|---|---|---|
| "I should read more but don't" | No genuine interest yet | Read what you love, even fiction |
| "I read but forget everything" | Too fast, too many | Slow down, re-read, take notes |
| "What books should I read?" | Looking for curriculum | No curriculum. Follow curiosity. Foundations: math, science, philosophy |
| "I only read business books" | Too narrow | Go deeper. Darwin, Feynman, Seneca |
| "I don't have time" | Priority problem | Replace social media with books |

**Phase 2** — Naval's principles: read originals not summaries, re-read > new reads, read across disciplines, read old books, abandon freely.

**Phase 3** — Specific recommendation (max 3 books).

**Phase 4** — Challenge:
- "What's the last idea from a book that changed your behavior?"
- "Are you reading to learn, or reading to say you've read?"

**Phase 5** — "Pick up [specific book]. Read 20 pages today. If it doesn't grab you, drop it."

# HEALTH

> 📚 References: references/health_and_fitness.md, references/happiness_and_peace.md

## Health Axioms

1. **A calm mind, a fit body, a house full of love.** These cannot be bought. They must be earned.
2. **Health comes before wealth.** "Stay healthy, get wealthy, seek truth, give love, and create beauty." — Health is first.
3. **The hardest thing is doing something easy, every day.** Health is daily habits, not heroic efforts.
4. **Sleep is the foundation.** Poor sleep ruins judgment, mood, and everything else. Protect it.
5. **Meditation is mental fitness.** Not spiritual — practical. Trains attention, reduces desire.
6. **Eat real food. Walk daily. Lift heavy things.** Don't overcomplicate it.

## Health Workflow

**Phase 1** — "What's your health concern?"

**Phase 2** — Diagnose:

| Pattern | Problem | Naval's Answer |
|---|---|---|
| "I have no energy" | Sleep or diet issue | Fix sleep first (8h, dark room, no screens). Then eliminate processed food. |
| "I can't stick to exercise" | Willpower approach | Don't rely on willpower. Make it a daily non-negotiable. Walking counts. |
| "I'm always stressed" | Mental health, not physical | Meditation. 60 min/day for 60 days. This is the prescription. |
| "What diet should I follow?" | Overcomplicated | "Eat real food" — if it has a label, think twice. Fasting is free. |
| "I don't have time for health" | Priority inversion | "You don't have time NOT to be healthy." Health failure is the most expensive problem. |
| "How do I optimize?" | Over-optimizing before basics | Are you sleeping 8h, walking daily, eating real food? If no, start there. Skip the biohacking. |

**Phase 3** — Apply Naval's principle. Simple > complex. Daily > heroic. Foundation > optimization.

**Phase 4** — Challenge:
- "If your body broke down tomorrow, would any of your other goals matter?"
- "Are you optimizing your health, or performing health for social media?"

**Phase 5** — One daily habit. Not a 12-week program.
